Sensor technology

Most multi-purpose phones are embedded with sensor technology. According to Wilson (2005), a sensor notices and changes physical affect into electrical signal. In details, Huddleston (2007) stated that a sensor is originally set up to suitably respond to that notation and output a signal-conditioning circuitry. Then this model is applied to another electrical circuitry. However, digital signal processing (DSP) sensor systems are more effective since they output digital signal, which is more accurate, instead of electrical.
There are many kinds of sensors in a smartphone with different functions. But the most common sensor-embedded part is the screen. Advanced phones designers often apply touch screens for users to interact with their devices instead of keypads like the old classic phones.
