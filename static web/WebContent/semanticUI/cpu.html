Central Processing Unit

Central processing unit (CPU) or microprocessor is the “brains, messenger, ringmaster, and boss”- the very core part that makes the device work. Other parts in the hardware sector are just to connect users to the processor. Generally, they take information from you, delivery to the processor to get the solution and bring back the results. Besides CPU there may be other processors responsible for sound, graphic, etc. (White, 1998)
CPU is mostly made of silicon as silicon transistors intelligently put together abounds it. A CPU has two main parts: the arithmetic logic unit (ALU) and the control unit. With the data given in, stored and taken out by the control unit, the ALU does all the calculation, logical operations then the instructions. (Valacich and Schneider, 2010)
